Expansion of the "greenhouse effect" Long-lived gases that remain semi-permanently in the atmosphere and do not respond physically or chemically to changes in temperature are described as "forcing" climate change. Gases, such as water vapor, which respond physically or chemically to changes in temperature are seen as "feedbacks." CO2 concentration by 48% since the Industrial Revolution began. This is the most important long-lived "forcing" of climate change. Greenhouse gases have been increasing because of human activities. These gases include Carbon dioxide, Water vapor, Methane, and many more. Because of increased human activity these gases are becoming more common in the case of carbon dioxide. There is 48% more of it in the atmosphere than there was at the beginning of the industrial revolution.
